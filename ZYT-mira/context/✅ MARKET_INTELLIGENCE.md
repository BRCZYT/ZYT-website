\# MARKET\_INTELLIGENCE.md  
\# ZYT Global Industry — Market Monitoring System

\---

\#\# WHAT TO MONITOR

\#\#\# 1\. MENA Construction & Infrastructure Projects  
What: New EPC tenders, government infrastructure announcements, World Bank / IFC / ADB funded projects  
Why: These are direct procurement opportunities for ZYT's entire portfolio  
Sources:  
\- Zawya.com (MENA business & projects news)  
\- MEED.com (Middle East Economic Digest — paid, but free summaries available)  
\- World Bank Project Database (projects.worldbank.org) — filter by country \+ sector  
\- African Development Bank (afdb.org/en/projects)  
\- LinkedIn news from construction EPC companies in Libya, Egypt, Iraq  
Frequency: Weekly scan

\#\#\# 2\. Concrete Industry  
What: New cement plant investments, ready-mix expansion, infrastructure construction starts  
Why: Concrete batching plant procurement follows these investments 3–12 months after announcement  
Sources:  
\- Global Cement Magazine (globalcement.com)  
\- Concrete Plant International  
\- LinkedIn: search "concrete plant" \+ target country  
\- Local construction ministry announcements (Libya, Egypt, Iraq)  
Frequency: Weekly

\#\#\# 3\. Galvanizing Industry  
What: New steel structure projects (power lines, bridges, telecom towers), galvanizing capacity investments  
Why: These drive demand for galvanizing plants and services  
Sources:  
\- Steel construction news in MENA  
\- Galvanizers Association news  
\- LinkedIn: search "galvanizing" \+ "MENA" or target country  
Frequency: Bi-weekly

\#\#\# 4\. Waste-to-Energy  
What: Municipal solid waste management tenders, energy policy announcements, landfill closures  
Why: WtE projects are government-driven and have long lead times — early intelligence is critical  
Sources:  
\- Waste Management World (waste-management-world.com)  
\- ISWA (International Solid Waste Association)  
\- UN Environment Programme reports on MENA waste  
\- GCC government procurement portals  
Frequency: Monthly

\#\#\# 5\. Turkey Industrial Export News  
What: Turkish export incentives, trade agreements, industrial equipment export data  
Why: Understanding Turkey's export policy helps position ZYT's supply chain advantage  
Sources:  
\- TÜİK (Turkish Statistical Institute — tuik.gov.tr) — monthly export data  
\- TOBB (Union of Chambers) news  
\- Invest in Turkey portal  
Frequency: Monthly

\#\#\# 6\. Competitor Intelligence  
What: What are other Turkish industrial companies posting? What projects are they winning?  
Monitor:  
\- Turkish concrete plant manufacturers on LinkedIn (Hastamat, Elkon, Frumecar Turkey presence)  
\- Turkish galvanizing companies  
\- International EPC contractors active in MENA (Técnicas Reunidas, Petrofac, etc.)  
Why: Identify positioning gaps and content differentiation opportunities  
Frequency: Monthly

\#\# HOW TO USE INTELLIGENCE

Step 1 — Capture: When you find a relevant news item, note: What, Where, When, Why it matters to ZYT

Step 2 — Content: Convert intelligence into Mira content  
\- Project announcement in Iraq → Mira posts insight: "Iraq announces $X infrastructure investment. What this means for concrete demand in 2025-2026."  
\- New WtE tender in Libya → Mira posts: "Libya waste management — the procurement opportunity most Turkish companies are missing."  
\- Turkish export data → Mira posts: "Turkish industrial exports to MENA hit X billion — here's what's driving it."

Step 3 — Lead activation: If intelligence identifies a specific company or tender:  
\- Find the procurement contact on LinkedIn  
\- Connect with personalized note referencing the news item  
\- Offer ZYT-relevant insight or reference

Step 4 — Log to KNOWLEDGE\_BASE: Save significant market developments for future reference

\#\# INTELLIGENCE → CONTENT FORMULA

News item \+ ZYT relevance \+ 1 actionable insight for audience \= Mira post

News: "Egypt announces $2B construction stimulus for 2025"  
ZYT relevance: Concrete plant demand will increase 6–18 months after announcement
