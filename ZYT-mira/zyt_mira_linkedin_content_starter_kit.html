3 LinkedIn 2. Hafta Beton / Teknik
Why do concrete batching plants in Libya and Egypt fail within 3 years? — Mira, ZYT Industry Analyst It's rarely the machine. It's the spec. The three most common mismatches we see: 1. Capacity over-specified A 120 m³/h plant ordered for a project that needs 60 m³/h peak. Result: the plant runs at 50% load, components wear unevenly, maintenance becomes unpredictable. 2. Aggregate moisture not accounted for MENA aggregates often have higher moisture variability than the plant was calibrated for. No moisture compensation system = inconsistent mix quality. 3. No commissioning support in-country The plant arrives, gets assembled, and the supplier leaves. Operators are trained for 2 days. 6 months later, calibration drifts and nobody knows why. A batching plant is a 15-year investment. The spec matters more than the price. We've commissioned plants across the region. Happy to answer technical questions below. #ConcreteBatchingPlant #ConcreteIndustry #MENA #Libya #Egypt #Construction #ZYTBeton
4 LinkedIn 2. Hafta Galvaniz / Teknik
Hot-dip galvanizing: what buyers in infrastructure projects get wrong. — Mira, ZYT Industry Analyst Galvanizing is specified by the engineer. Bought by procurement. Installed by the contractor. And nobody in the chain has seen the inside of a galvanizing plant. Here's what gets missed: The coating thickness is not just a quality indicator — it's a service life predictor. EN 1461 specifies minimum coating thickness by material type. A 45 μm coating on structural steel in a coastal environment (Libya, Alexandria, Basra) gives you maybe 15 years. 85 μm gives you 40+. The difference in cost? About 8–12% of the galvanizing price. The difference in lifecycle cost? Orders of magnitude. At ZYT Galvaniz, we help clients spec correctly before commissioning a galvanizing facility — or help them evaluate galvanizing service providers before placing orders.
